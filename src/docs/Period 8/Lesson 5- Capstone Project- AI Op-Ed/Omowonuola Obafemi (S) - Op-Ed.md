**Title: When AI Uses Basketball During Games and Practices.**

Op/Ed: **The Hook : AI has been added to basketball mostly during games. It’s used for possession of the basketball, to track passes, rebounds, dribbles, and shots, it can also be used to automatically deliver highlight reels.**

**Main  Argument : Personally I think AI could be a good use in basketball. Because it can help teams get better, and know what things that they need to get better, practice at, or even improve strategies on the court. The NBA is even using AI for  advanced scouting. AI in basketball relies on sophisticated computer vision systems, wearable sensors, tracking cameras, and machine learning algorithms to analyze player movements, detect fouls, predict outcomes, and optimize performance.** 

**Why  Should  People  Believe  Me : Because I play basketball, I know that there are definitely times where the referees have called foul shots or something like that because they THINK they saw someone push or grab someone. Sometimes the referees are WRONG, which makes the game start to become unfair. Another example is when someone on your team is taking 1 foul shot, and the ref says one of your other teammates went in for the ball too early, then the ball gets rewarded to the other team, when in reality it didn’t. AI is able to detect issues like these more precisely. I bet even famous players like Steph Curry, or Lebron James, and Jason Tatum, have been through issues like this.** 

**Point  Three \- Based  On  A True Story: Two New Jersey teams were playing at the 5th quarter and the other team shot the winning point, but later it was revealed that the refs decided not to count the point because ‘apparently the basket went in after the buzzer’. Now the school is facing legal action because the basket did in fact actually go in before the buzzer. If they had used some AI to analyze that game the refs would have seen the basket shot would have been counted, no questions asked.**

**The  Other  Side :  While the other side has good concerns like the AI being biased, athlete privacy, data ethics, ethical concerns, and fairness. They're also good points. While I am saying that AI is good in basketball, it doesn't mean that it can't also improve.**

**Concussion: Personally I feel like AI is a really good use in basketball, like highlight reels without manual editing, fouls etc. there are bad sides to it too, like fairness and being biased etc.**
