# The AI Age Verification Illusion: Why Facial Recognition Can't Keep Kids Safe Online

# 

Just imagine yourself going to Discord to chat with your unemployed friends, and before you login in, it hits you: “YOU MUST SCAN YOUR FACE-AI WILL IDENTIFY YOUR AGE” The problem is, you’re 6 years old, and your unemployed friends are over 19 years. If you just scan your normal face, you will automatically get banned until you are 18 and above. That’s like 12 years to go\! And since you are impatient, you do something diabolical…

AI powered face verification actually sounds convenient, even futuristic. The main goal of this AI is simple: keep children safe online. You have probably seen tech companies saying their AI age verification is very accurate and puts you in age groups(for example, age groups under 13, 13-17, 18+). I scanned my face on roblox and it placed me in age group 10\. It’s kind of a close approximation since I am 14 years old. The thing that no one tells you is that the AI is likely flawed. Who knows? There are at least 100 ways that AI does not work.

Here’s how AI age verification works: The AI scans your face and converts every pixel into complex numerical patterns. The system locates important details, like position of pupils, corner of the eyes, and lip size. It also checks your face, like freckles, hair color, facial texture, and compares it with millions of diverse images of people. It then goes through all age categories and sees which one you pass in. There are other ways AI can identify your age, such as how old you are, account history, etc.  You can think of it as the Sorting Hat(if you ever heard of Harry Potter). He assigns you to either Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, or Slytherin based on your personality and capabilities.

However, there is one problem. It doesn’t actually tell your age. It simply makes an educated guess based on how old you look, and sometimes it can be chaotic. Technology struggles with various things, like genetics. For example, a 16 year old might look 6 and a 6 year old might look 16\. Genetics, ethnicity, and environmental factors all affect how old someone really looks. Makeup, light, camera angle, and image quality can dramatically affect AI age verification.

According to scientific studies, these systems have many significant errors on certain demographics. In fact, the Algorithmic Justice League, a non-profit organization about AI safety, discovered that the AI was worse about women(sorry) and people with darker skin, so AI might be less accurate on these groups. A 15 year old might be classified as an adult, but a young looking 20 year old might not fit in any categories.

Companies love to brag about how their ai facial age estimation is 95% accurate. Actually, I think we should do the math and see what it actually means: If some game platform has like 100 million users and the AI age verification is 95%, then 5 million users have been misclassified. Suppose a fraction of those 5 million are children granted adult access and predatory spaces, then it could be 100,000 \+ children at risks. But the real accuracy rates are not even close than advertised, as those 95% figures typically come from controlled testing environments such as test subjects and high quality images. The environments in the real world are low quality, bad lighting, and uncooperated subjects. A 2023 study from the University of Toronto found that popular age verifications are misclassified by 3+ years in 20% of all cases. Another investigation found that minors somehow got access to restricted contact 30-40% of the time by simple workarounds.  
So kids are annoyed by this new feature, and they decided enough is enough. They fear that if they use their own face, they’ll be blocked from chat, voice chat, or pretty much anything, so here some ways people outsmarted the AI: please don’t do this

* Pictures of other faces: this is the simplest method: they can use photos of older siblings, parents, or even random people. Many systems online don’t align the same photo with the same person using the account, and some do not even require live photos.  
* The makeup trick: teenage girls discovered that by adding makeup on the forehead, simulating wrinkles, and strategic lighting, they can boost their age output. I seen a video of someone using their finger white a smiley face and they were 21+  
* AI deepfake: YOu can take a picture of yourself and ask AI to edit it and make it look older. 

Here are 3 predictions that i think would be possible 

1. I think that in 100 years, AI age verification will be so extremely accurate that it’ll be up to molecular analysis for extremely accurate results. AI might even use the iris pattern, heartbeat, and how you walk. AI actually tracks your health and it could contribute to this improvement.  
2. I think in 100 years, AI can do molecular analysis to identify if it’s looking at organic human skin or simply pixel cells or makeup. If you remember the finger with a smiley face, maybe AI will be trained to identify human face vs dog face vs fish face vs other things. Before, archerfish(Toxotes jaculatrix) could identify at least 100 human faces with 86-88% accuracy. AI now holds the title with 99% accuracy.  
3. Due to current privacy issues, AI can now use zero knowledge proof, so you can automatically be 18 years old without ever showing your date.  
   However, there’s still hope, and we can make it better and more accurate. How? Well in the real world, we have bad camera quality and crappy light. If it’s dark, then we can add a bright light so the details are clear and present. We can also make the AI adapt to identifying some dark images and bad quality. It can predict that even if the nose is not clearly detailed, the shape around it can still mean it’s either a kid or adult. To make it extra safe, maybe we can make AI ask parents for clearer details like the birth certificate, real age, and other personal information.  
     
   Ultimately, AI sounds like a good and safe feature to classify and categorize users, and that they can interact and chat with other users in the same age group. However, the AI is not 100% accurate, as it can misclassify users into age groups where they should not belong, and it’s all controlled by genetics, camera quality, lighting, etc. The reason why companies say it’s accurate is only because they used high quality images in great lighting. It’s not our fault- AI wasn’t prepared for situations like this. Also many people bypassed and fooled the AI, almost making it unreliable. However, if we can show AI normal quality pictures and try its best to figure out the age based on facial details, and let the parents do the job, maybe there is a chance where we can make a safe platform and enjoy no danger at all.
