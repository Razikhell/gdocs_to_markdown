**Google AI Overview’s Effect On Everyday Thinking**

	Google AI Overview is something that is rapidly becoming part of our everyday life and contributing to the way research is done. Imagine asking a question and having someone whisper the answer in your ear before you even finish speaking—no need to think it through yourself. This is very similar to what Google AI Overview does. In less than a second, Google's AI can scan thousands of sources, analyze complex information, and deliver a confident answer to almost any question you ask. While this technology feels like having a genius assistant at our fingertips, these AI summaries are becoming our default source of truth. As this technology advances, we must ask ourselves whether we're gaining convenience or losing independence in our brain.

	Google AI Overview is limiting humanity's ability to independently discover and evaluate information. The process of finding information was originally similar to solving a puzzle—you search, compare sources, figure out if these sources are correct, and piece together your own understanding. This brain "game" strengthens critical thinking skills, determines the real versus the fake information, and builds confidence in our ability to understand how to decipher the complex information that we find. However, AI Overview eliminates this entire process by simply handing us the final answer, like a player who completes a video game for you while you watch. Yes, it's faster and easier, but you never learn the controls, develop the strategies, or experience the satisfaction of solving challenges yourself. When AI consistently plays the information-gathering game for us, our brains weaken and falter in crucial situations, we forget how to get to the end of the game on our own. The way of instant AI-generated answers is convenient and easy, but it may come with the price of our own ability to decipher critical situations and information.

	To back this idea up how our brain progressively becomes less effective when repeatedly using AI, a neuroscientist, Dr. Maryanne Wolf from UCLA, has something to say about that. She says that when you use AI consistently, the regions of the brain that are responsible for analyzing information, reading deeply,  and comparing ideas, become weak. Our brain adapts to the task that we are regularly performing. So, when AI does all the work for us that the brain is supposed to do, it creates a sort-of rewiring in our brain. Though AI is saving us time, it is diminishing our ability to think for ourselves.

	There is more on how our brain changes due to Google AI overview, especially in the lives of students. Sarah Chen, a high school librarian in Portland, noticed that many of her students could not figure out whether a source was a peer-reviewed journal, a blog post, or a commercial website. A similar result was found when she asked her juniors to find credible sources. Entire generations are growing up, learning that they can find much of what they need from AI Overview. They depend on the summaries to guide them to a concrete answer, or that is what they think. This creates a new generation that takes answers and doesn’t really question them, which could result in bad decisions.

	When we keep depending on AI Overview, it is creating an illusion that anything the AI says is the real, accurate answer. For example, Marcus, a college sophomore, used AI Overviews to study for his biology exam. The AI overview gave him explanations, definitions, and other information he wanted.  But when the exam wanted Marcus to apply his knowledge, Marcus got lost. This is because he never tried for himself to figure out the information, to go through the trial and error of what was correct and how he went wrong. This could happen to anybody, students and the general public. More people will feel as though they have the right answer, that they know something,  but in reality, they have no idea how to prove it, or challenge it.

	Though all these points show how negatively Google AI Overview is impacting our brain and cognitive thinking, there are some good sides. The Overview gives you a strong, clear, answer that could help you when you really want to know something really quickly. If you want a place to find sources, Google AI shows you the sources that it pulled information from. On the bright side, Google AI can be very helpful, make some good questions a lot easier to comprehend and give faster answers.   
	  
	My conclusion is that Google AI Overview will continue to be part of our everyday life, whether we want it or not. Something I would recommend though, would be to not use Google AI Overview repetitively, especially for a certain project, or something important, maybe for school or work. If you use it, I completely understand, I have used AI Overview to answer many questions that I have had. In the end, it is all about how much you want to rely on the answers of Google AI, and how much you think for yourself on whether it is true.
