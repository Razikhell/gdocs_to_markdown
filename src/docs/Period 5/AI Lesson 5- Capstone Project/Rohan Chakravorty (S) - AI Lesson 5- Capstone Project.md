     What is Grammerly AI and What is It to Us

       
Every day, over 30 million people start typing emails, essays, and text messages while an AI watches every word they write, quietly fixing their mistakes before anyone else sees them. This AI is called Grammarly, and since it launched in 2009, it has checked over 100 billion words. But have you ever stopped to think about what happens when an entire generation learns to write with an AI looking over their shoulder? I would argue that Grammarly AI is quietly changing how we write and communicate. As a society, we need to understand these changes and take steps to protect our ability to write on our own.

The old way of becoming a good writer was tough. You had to make mistakes, get feedback from teachers, fix your work, and slowly get better over time. Grammarly AI is changing that. The AI does not make you think about your mistakes. It just fixes them automatically. When your errors disappear before you even finish typing, you never learn why those mistakes were wrong in the first place. Grammarly has helped millions of people write cleaner sentences instantly. But here is the problem. A 2022 study found that students who relied heavily on writing AI tools showed 34% less improvement in grammar skills over a semester compared to students who got traditional feedback from teachers. That is not all Grammarly is doing. In 2023, 68% of high school English teachers said their students have become dependent on AI writing tools and cannot edit their own work anymore. We are creating a generation that can write perfect essays with AI help but cannot write a clean paragraph when the technology is not there.

Here is where things get unfair. Grammarly's basic version is free, but the premium features cost30,000 do not have internet at home. This makes it nearly impossible to use AI writing tools regularly. To make matters worse, a 2023 survey found that 67% of hiring managers said they are more impressed by well written job applications. This means that access to premium AI tools is literally deciding who gets opportunities and who does not.

Perhaps the most troubling change is how Grammarly AI is making everyone's writing sound identical. The AI is trained on huge amounts of data that reflect one specific style of English. Usually formal and business like. When millions of people accept the same AI's suggestions, we risk making all writing sound the same. Over time, this creates a loop. Writing that fits the algorithm's patterns gets praised more, which means more people copy that style, which means the AI recommends it even more. Meanwhile, unique voices get marked as errors that need fixing. This is what experts call a filter bubble. To make matters worse, Grammarly often marks African American Vernacular English and regional expressions like y'all as mistakes. A 2023 study from Stanford found that AI writing tools unfairly correct non standard English, basically pressuring users to write in one narrow way. If everyone is letting the same AI edit their writing, everyone's writing will begin to sound the same.

It is important to admit that Grammarly is not all bad. People still learn to write through practice, reading, and feedback from real teachers. Additionally, some would argue that having clearer communication across society could actually help us understand each other better. For people with dyslexia and people learning English, these tools provide real help. A 2023 survey found that 78% of people with dyslexia said AI writing assistants made them feel less anxious about writing. Plus, if you really want to develop your writing skills, you can always practice writing without the help of AI.

Grammarly AI is more than just a convenient tool. It is a powerful force that is changing who succeeds in school and work, threatening our ability to write on our own, and potentially making everyone's writing sound the same. Like any powerful tool, it can be used for good or for harm. So what can we do? We cannot stop algorithms from existing, but we can make sure we do not let them replace our own abilities. I think that schools should make certain assignments AI free zones where students must show they can write without help. Imagine having to write an essay in class without any AI assistance. It would force us to actually learn the skills instead of relying on technology\!

Until schools add that requirement, we can do it ourselves. Challenge yourself to write something without AI this week\! Turn off Grammarly and write a journal entry, a letter to a friend, or even just a long text message. Make mistakes, learn from them, and share your unedited writing with classmates. Let's practice writing independently together. You might just discover that your own voice, mistakes and all, is more interesting than anything an algorithm could create.
